  \begin{enumerate}
    \item Let $T$ be continuous. Then for fixed $y\in Y$, the map $T(\cdot,y)\colon X\to Z$ is continuous: Let $i_y\colon X\to X\times Y$ be the inclusion map sending $x\in X$ to $(x,y)$, which is \textit{not} linear. The map $i_y$ is continuous since preimages under $i_y$ of the open sets $U\times V$ in $X\times Y$ for $U\subset X$, $V\subset Y$ open are either the empty set or $U$. It follows that $T(\cdot,y) = T\circ i_y$ is continuous, and hence bounded so that $\norm{T(x,y)}\leq \norm{T(\cdot,y)}\norm{x}$ for all $x\in X$. Similarly, for fixed $x\in X$, the map $T(x,\cdot)$ is bounded with $\norm{T(x,y)}\leq \norm{T(x,\cdot)}\norm{y}$
